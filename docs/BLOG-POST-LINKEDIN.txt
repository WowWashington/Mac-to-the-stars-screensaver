I asked Claude to build a Mac screensaver. It built me a universe.

I've been a Windows guy forever (with plenty of *nix), but I set up a Mac Mini Pro for one reason: to build with — and abuse — Claude and other AI tools. Before pointing it at real work, I wanted a calibration test with real moving parts: native macOS APIs, GPU code, system integration.

The test: "Build a procedural screensaver where I fly through galaxies, past planets, with warp jumps between them. Then install it." No Xcode project, no template, no assets. Go.

One session later, Claude's Fable model delivered the coolest screensaver I've ever run:

• The Milky Way growing from a single pixel (real NASA imagery) into a volumetric, ray-marched galactic disk you fly through, mini solar systems drifting past
• Flybys of procedural planets — terran, ringed gas giants, lava, ice — lit by their own suns, sometimes binary pairs
• Star Wars-style warp jumps into new regions
• Rare encounters: lensing black holes, comet swarms, and Dyson spheres in three build stages — including one you fly INTO, skimming the oceans and mountain ranges of its inner surface
• Optional starship HUD: velocity, heading, sectors, warp telemetry
• NASA deep-field photos, properly credited and logo-free

All generated live by one Metal shader. No video files. No two runs the same.

What impressed me most was watching it work. It built its own test harness, looked at its own rendered frames, and iterated: "nebula too foggy" — fix, re-render, check. When I reported stars flying the wrong way, it found a sign error in the projection math and fixed it everywhere. It benchmarked the GPU per scene to hold 60fps, and even caught macOS silently reverting my second monitor to a built-in saver — then wrote a script so it can't happen again.

Fit and finish remains, but it's fully running today: dual screen, HUD on/off, planets sliding by, warp tunnels lighting up the office — for about 90% of one 5-hour plan window.

Lifelong Windows/*nix person, Mac bought to stress-test AI: test passed.
